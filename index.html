First Aid Preparedness

Learn essential first aid steps to help in emergency situations. Scroll through key practices to keep your community safe.

Bleeding Control

  • Apply direct pressure to the wound with a clean cloth.
  • Elevate the injured area above the heart if possible.
  • If bleeding is severe, call for emergency medical help immediately.
  • Do not remove any objects impaled in the wound.

Burn Treatment

  • Cool the burn with cool (not ice-cold) running water for 10-20 minutes.
  • Remove jewelry or tight clothing near the burn area.
  • Cover the burn with a sterile non-stick bandage or clean cloth.
  • Do not apply ointments, butter, or oils to severe burns. Seek medical attention.

Fracture Care

  • Immobilize the injured area; do not try to realign the bone.
  • Apply a splint if trained, extending beyond the joints above and below the fracture.
  • Apply ice packs to reduce swelling and pain.
  • Seek immediate medical attention.

Choking Aid

  • Ask "Are you choking?" If the person can cough or speak, encourage coughing.
  • If the person cannot speak, breathe, or cough: Perform 5 back blows between the shoulder blades.
  • Follow with 5 abdominal thrusts (Heimlich maneuver).
  • Repeat sets of 5 back blows and 5 abdominal thrusts until the object is expelled or help arrives.

Heat Stroke

  • Move the person to a cool, shaded area immediately.
  • Loosen or remove restrictive clothing.
  • Cool the person rapidly: apply cool, wet cloths or spray with cool water. Fan the person.
  • Offer sips of cool water if conscious and able to swallow.
  • Call emergency medical services immediately; heatstroke is life-threatening.

Nosebleed Care

  • Have the person sit upright and lean forward slightly.
  • Pinch the soft part of the nose (nostrils) firmly for 10-15 minutes.
  • Breathe through the mouth. Apply a cold compress to the bridge of the nose.
  • If bleeding persists after 20 minutes, or is due to injury, seek medical help.

Poisoning Emergency

  • Call your local poison control center or emergency services immediately.
  • Try to identify the poison; look for containers or clues.
  • Do NOT induce vomiting unless instructed by a medical professional.
  • If the person is unconscious or having trouble breathing, call emergency services.

Electric Shock

  • Do NOT touch the person if they are still in contact with the electrical source.
  • Turn off the power source if possible, or use a non-conductive object (e.g., dry wood) to move the source away.
  • Check for breathing and pulse. Begin CPR if trained and necessary.
  • Call emergency medical services immediately. Treat for shock.

CPR (Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation)

  • Check the person's responsiveness and breathing. Call for help if unresponsive.
  • Place the person on a firm, flat surface.
  • Begin chest compressions: Place your hands in the center of the chest and press hard and fast, at least 2 inches deep and 100-120 compressions per minute.
  • After 30 compressions, give 2 rescue breaths if trained to do so. Otherwise, continue compressions.
  • Continue CPR until emergency responders arrive or the person shows signs of life.
